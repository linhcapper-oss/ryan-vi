Upgrade Sentences and Vocabulary

This worksheet helps students move from very basic Task 1 sentences to more flexible structures. It focuses on vocabulary, model sentence patterns, controlled rewriting practice, and three simple samples for upgrading.

Suggested teaching flow
1. Teach key vocabulary first.
2. Show the difference between basic and upgraded structures.
3. Let students rewrite short sentences with support.
4. Ask students to improve the three very simple samples.

For Tables
The table compares the number of exchange students between Europe and Australia in 2007 and 2009.
Overall, the total number of students going from Europe to Australia increased, while the reverse figure remained stable.
Germany recorded the most noticeable rise over the period.
For Bar Charts
The bar charts illustrate the distribution of employment in three sectors in three countries in 1980 and 2020.
Overall, services accounted for the largest share in Countries A and C, while agriculture was dominant in Country B in 1980.
The service sector expanded in each country by 2020.
For Line Graphs
The line graph shows the level of satisfaction with the healthcare system in four countries between 1991 and 2007.
Overall, the Netherlands remained relatively stable at a high level, whereas the US stayed much lower than the other countries.
Canada experienced the most dramatic fluctuation during the period.
